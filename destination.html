Tourist Spots

Our Lady's Garden

Directions

When it comes to the lush greeneries, a great ocean, and mountain view…look no further! Our Lady's Garden in Sibulan is a place “Where the Mountain Meets the Sea” and where the big statue of Mother Mary overlooks the water of the Tañon straight and the island of Cebu with her protective spirits. On the other side, the majestic mountainous landscape of Negros Oriental can be seen. This attraction is open for all to meditate and unwind to. Being elevated a couple of meters from the main highway, a breath of fresh air can give an ultimate relaxing sensation to the day trip.

Address: Sibulan, Negros Oriental 6201 Philippines

Facebook Page:https://www.facebook.com/ourladysgarden

Puncak Tanawan

Directions

Puncak is known as a popular hiking site in the island of Negros Oriental. It is said to be the highest peak in Sibulan, located specifically in Brgy. Balugo. According to their official Facebook page, Puncak Tanawan Inc., their mission is to “develop an attraction not only to entice visitors, but also to raise awareness where visitors can see the beauty of nature.”

Address: Puncak Tanawan, Balugo, Sibulan, Negros Oriental 6201 Philippines

Facebook Page:https://www.facebook.com/PuncakTanawan

Tuktok Campsite

Directions

Tuktok Campsite is a picnic ground and a campsite. One of the wonderful destination of Sibulan Negros Oriental. If you love camping and want to have a picnic with your family and friends, Tuktok Campsite is good for you. It is located in Cansal-ing, Ajong, Sibulan. At the hill-top, you could see the panoramic views of the land below and Cebu Island is visible. The place is perfect for unwinding, relaxing and enjoying the simplicity of nature.

Address: Casal-ing, Ajong Sibulan, Negros Oriental 6201 Philippines

Facebook Page:https://www.facebook.com/tuktokcampsite

San Antonio de Padua Sibulan Parish

Directions

Dedicated to St. Anthony of Padua, this church of Sibulan was the third built on site since the founding of the parish in the mid 19th century. A great number of St. Anthony devotees from all over Negros Oriental crowd at the Sibulan church to light candles for the patron saint every 13th of each month especially during his feastday, transforming the church grounds and the plaza fronting it into a bustling festive gathering. A fluvial procession is also held annually June 13, his feast day.

Address: Osmena st. Poblacion, Sibulan, Negros Oriental 6201 Philippines

Facebook Page:https://www.facebook.com/san.antonio.of.sibulan

Balinsasayao and Danao Twin Lake

Directions

One of the most visited Tourist Spots in Negros Oriental are the Twin Lakes of Sibulan. It is composed of two small but deep fresh water lakes called Lake Balinsasayao and Lake Danao. On a clear day, the blue of the sky is reflected in the water of the Twin Lakes, giving a perfect atmosphere for relaxation. The lakes are embraced on all sides by narrow mountains thick with exotic plant-life. When mist obscures the mountains, the area looks like something out of a fairytale or legend. Just like traveling to a mystical land in a story, getting to the Twin Lakes is something of an adventure. Once a visitor arrives, the relaxing atmosphere will wash away any unwanted stress.

Address: San Jose, Sibulan, Negros Oriental 6201 Philippines

Facebook Page:https://www.facebook.com/balinsasayaotwinlakes
